11: BROKEN BLADE

I’m beginning to think courtly love
isn’t my speed. The other cavaliers
in my regiment would wade
into darkness for the gentle slope

of our lady’s neck, the fringe that kisses
her brow. I pass no judgment
over dogs; they are sweet in their
blind devotion. Regrettably,

I am harder to satisfy. Combat
irritates me. I grow weary of blood
and metal sounds. Fantasies
of perfect, perfumed hands upon my

mangled helm do not thrill me towards
suffering. Will not rescue me
from expiry. Call me a bad knight
if it pleases you. I’d rather

trade sleep’s cousin for a little
death. Shed my armor for the hard-won
calluses of the laundress
or the blacksmith. Bow my head

between bruised knees and
pray. Angels rolling
in the dirt with me, lips
as hungry as mine.
